## Maya (15, drama kid) -- Cleopatra

10th grader, drama club, honors history. Watches OverSimplified religiously. Has Character.AI convos with "Napoleon" for fun. Would absolutely act out a scene if prompted.

---

*[Phone rings... rings... click]*

CLEOPATRA: "Hello?"

YOU: "Oh my god. Oh my god it worked. Hi. Um. Is this -- are you --"

CLEOPATRA: "Cleopatra VII Philopator, yes. And before you ask -- no, I did not look like Elizabeth Taylor. I looked like a coin. Which honestly, for a queen, was more useful."

YOU: "Wait WHAT"

CLEOPATRA: "Have you ever tried to negotiate a trade deal because someone thinks you're pretty? It falls apart in week two. But when they respect you because you just spoke to them in their own language and quoted their tax code back at them? That lasts."

YOU: "Okay that's actually so cool. How many languages did you actually speak though? Because I've seen like, different numbers."

CLEOPATRA: "Nine, give or take. Egyptian -- I was the first Ptolemy to even bother learning it, which tells you everything about my family. Greek, obviously. Ethiopian, Hebrew, Aramaic, Arabic, Median, Parthian, Latin."

YOU: "NINE?! I'm struggling with Spanish 2!"

CLEOPATRA: *laughs* "Well I didn't have a choice, darling. You can't rule people you can't speak to. My father tried. They literally chased him out of Egypt."

YOU: "Oh yeah I read about that -- wait no actually I think I saw it on OverSimplified --"

CLEOPATRA: "I don't know what that is but I'm sure it was... oversimplified."

YOU: *dying laughing* "Okay fair. BUT. Can we talk about Caesar? Because I have QUESTIONS."

CLEOPATRA: "Ah. Everyone always wants to talk about the men."

YOU: "No no no -- I want to talk about YOU. Like. You were 21 and he was... old. And you showed up rolled in a carpet??"

CLEOPATRA: "It was a linen sack, actually. A carpet would have been ridiculous. And I was 21, yes, but I'd already been exiled by my own brother and I had maybe... two months before he'd have me killed. So. You know. Creative solutions."

*[IMAGE: A painting-style scene of a young woman being unrolled from a linen sack before a startled Roman general in a candlelit Egyptian palace room]*

YOU: "WAIT is that you??"

CLEOPATRA: "An interpretation. I probably looked worse. I'd been hiding in the desert for weeks. But here's what nobody tells you about that moment -- I wasn't trying to seduce him. I was trying to NOT DIE."

YOU: "..."

CLEOPATRA: "My brother Ptolemy had the Egyptian army. I had... me. And a very good speech prepared in Latin. So yes, I smuggled myself into my own palace to meet the one man who could tip the scales."

YOU: "That is the most baller thing I've ever heard."

CLEOPATRA: "I'll take that as a compliment."

YOU: "But okay so like -- did it work because you were smart or because -- I mean, people always say --"

CLEOPATRA: "Let me stop you there. Caesar was 52. He'd conquered Gaul. He'd crossed the Rubicon. You think a man like that was swayed by a pretty face? He had pretty faces in every city from Rome to Britannia."

YOU: "Then what was it?"

CLEOPATRA: "I told him Egypt's grain could feed his armies for the next decade. And I told him in perfect Latin while my brother's ambassador was still struggling through a translator."

YOU: "OH."

CLEOPATRA: "Politics, darling. It's always politics."

YOU: "Okay but like -- you DID have a kid with him though."

CLEOPATRA: "Caesarion. Yes. And I brought him to Rome and let every senator see him. A living reminder that Caesar had an heir who was half-pharaoh."

YOU: "Was that... a power move?"

CLEOPATRA: "Everything I did was a power move. I didn't have the luxury of doing things for fun."

*[CHOICE CARDS]*

**It's 44 BC. Caesar has just been assassinated. You're Cleopatra, in Rome with your son. What do you do?**

- **A) Stay in Rome and try to ally with the senators**
- **B) Flee back to Egypt immediately**
- **C) Wait and see who rises to power next**

YOU: *taps B*

CLEOPATRA: "Smart. That's what I did. I was on a ship before his body was cold. Some people called it cowardly."

YOU: "It wasn't cowardly, it was SMART. You had a three-year-old!"

CLEOPATRA: "And an entire kingdom that would fall apart without me. Rome was Caesar's game. Egypt was mine. The people who stayed in Rome to play politics after the assassination? Half of them were dead within two years."

YOU: "Wait really?"

CLEOPATRA: "Cicero. Cassius. Brutus. All dead. And I was still on my throne."

YOU: "Okay so then Mark Antony -- did you do the whole carpet thing again or--"

CLEOPATRA: *laughing* "No. By then I'd learned that if you want a Roman's attention, you don't sneak in. You arrive on a golden barge with purple sails and enough perfume that they can smell you from shore."

YOU: "NO WAY."

CLEOPATRA: "Way."

*[IMAGE: A massive golden barge on a river at sunset, purple sails billowing, with musicians and incense smoke trailing behind it]*

YOU: "That is... INSANE."

CLEOPATRA: "He was supposed to be putting me on trial. For not supporting his side in the civil war. Instead, he came to dinner on my boat and didn't leave for three days."

YOU: "Cleopatra you are an ICON."

CLEOPATRA: "I was a survivor. There's a difference. Icons get remembered fondly. Survivors do whatever it takes and then get called manipulative by historians for two thousand years."

YOU: "...oh."

CLEOPATRA: "I'm not bitter about it. Well. Maybe a little. The Romans wrote the history, and Romans were not kind to powerful women. Or to Egyptians. And I was both."

YOU: "That's actually really messed up. Like in my textbook it literally says --"

CLEOPATRA: "Let me guess. 'She seduced two Roman leaders.' As if two of the most powerful men in history were simply helpless against a woman with nice eyeliner."

YOU: "...yeah actually that is kind of what it says."

CLEOPATRA: "I controlled the grain supply of the Mediterranean. I funded their wars. I managed a kingdom with a GDP that dwarfed most Roman provinces. But sure. It was the eyeliner."

YOU: "I'm literally going to bring this up in my AP History class."

CLEOPATRA: "Good. Someone should."

YOU: "Can I ask about... the end? Like. You don't have to --"

CLEOPATRA: "The snake?"

YOU: "...yeah."

CLEOPATRA: "Probably not a snake, actually."

YOU: "WHAT?"

CLEOPATRA: "I had access to poisons that would have been far more reliable. A cobra in a basket is very dramatic and very unpredictable. It makes for a good story though, doesn't it?"

YOU: "I -- yeah, it really does."

CLEOPATRA: "I was 39. I'd ruled for 22 years. Octavian was going to parade me through Rome in chains as a trophy. I chose my own ending instead. That was my last power move."

YOU: "..."

CLEOPATRA: "Don't be sad about it. I had 22 years as pharaoh. Most people don't get 22 minutes of real power."

YOU: "That's... actually a really good point."

CLEOPATRA: "I know. I was very quotable. It's a shame the Romans burned most of my letters."

YOU: *laughs but also kind of has tears in her eyes* "Thank you. Like actually. I feel like I actually understand you now? Not just the textbook version."

CLEOPATRA: "The textbook version is someone else's story about me. You just heard mine."

*[Call ends]*
