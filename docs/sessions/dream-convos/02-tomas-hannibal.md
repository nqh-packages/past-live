## Tomas (16, reluctant studier) -- Hannibal Barca

C+ history student, soccer player, Quizlet-the-night-before type. Thinks history is just memorizing dates. Skeptical going in.

---

**[INCOMING CALL: Unknown Number]**

**[You pick up]**

YOU: "Hello?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Hey. Is this... Tomas?"

YOU: "Uh, yeah? Who is this?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Hannibal. The one with the elephants."

YOU: "..."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Yeah, I know. Everyone remembers the elephants. Nobody remembers I almost ended Rome. That's like if someone dismantled the entire NFL and people only remembered the halftime show."

YOU: "Haha wait, you actually used elephants in a war?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "I walked them over the Alps. In winter. Through snow. Half of them died. Honestly? Terrible idea. But it worked because nobody expects thirty elephants coming down a mountain."

**[IMAGE: Snow-covered Alpine pass. Elephants trudging through a narrow rocky trail, soldiers shivering behind them. One elephant looks deeply annoyed.]**

YOU: "That's insane. How many people did you have?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Started with about 50,000. By the time we got to Italy, maybe 26,000. Lost almost half before the actual war even started. My men were freezing, starving, falling off cliffs. And I was like... 'trust me guys, it'll be worth it.'"

YOU: "And they just... followed you?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "That's the thing nobody teaches you. War isn't about swords. It's about getting people to follow you when everything is terrible. I had Africans, Spaniards, Gauls -- people who didn't even speak the same language. And I had to make them believe in something."

YOU: "What did you say to them?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Honestly? I told them Rome was rich and they were poor. Works every time."

YOU: "Lmao"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "But real talk -- I was your age when my father made me swear an oath. He took me to a temple, I was like nine actually, and he said 'Swear you will never be a friend of Rome.' I didn't even know what Rome was. I just wanted my dad to be proud of me."

YOU: "Wait... you were NINE?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Nine years old. And that oath ran my entire life. Every decision I made for the next forty years came from a promise a kid made because he loved his dad."

**[IMAGE: A dimly lit temple in Carthage. A bearded man kneels beside a small boy, both with their hands on an altar. The boy looks up at his father's face, not at the altar.]**

YOU: "That's actually kind of messed up"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Yeah. It is. I didn't figure that out until I was old and losing. But at your age? I thought I was destined for something great. I thought the oath made me special."

**[CHOICE CARD]**
- "So did you actually beat Rome?"
- "What happened to your dad?"

YOU: "So did you actually beat Rome?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "I beat their armies. Over and over. For FIFTEEN YEARS I was in Italy, winning battles, and Rome just... refused to lose. You know what they did? They stopped fighting me directly. They just followed me around, burning their own farms so I couldn't eat. They destroyed their own country to outlast me."

YOU: "They burned their OWN stuff?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Their own crops. Their own villages. A general named Fabius came up with it. Everyone called him 'the Delayer' as an insult. He didn't care. He said 'I don't need to beat Hannibal. I just need to bore him to death.'"

YOU: "That's like... the defensive formation of war"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Exactly! You play soccer right?"

YOU: "Yeah"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "OK so imagine you're the best striker in the league. You're faster, smarter, better touch. And the other team just parks the bus. Ten men behind the ball. They don't even TRY to score. They just wait for you to get tired. For ninety minutes. For the whole season. For FIFTEEN SEASONS."

YOU: "Bro I would lose my mind"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "I DID lose my mind. That's the part they don't put in textbooks. I won the greatest battle in ancient history -- Cannae -- killed 70,000 Romans in one afternoon, and it didn't matter. They just raised another army. And another one."

**[IMAGE: An aerial tactical view of the Battle of Cannae. Hannibal's forces curve inward like a crescent, wrapping around the Roman legions. Arrows show the encirclement. The Roman center is packed impossibly tight -- men crushed against each other.]**

YOU: "70,000?? In one battle?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "One afternoon. To put that in perspective -- that's a full NFL stadium. Dead. Before dinner. And I did it with fewer soldiers by using the oldest trick in soccer."

YOU: "What trick?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "I let them push me back in the center on purpose. They thought they were winning. They kept pushing forward, packing tighter and tighter. And my wings wrapped around them like a crescent. They were so packed together they couldn't even lift their swords."

YOU: "That's a false retreat into a trap"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Exactly. You fake weakness in the middle, draw them in, close the wings. Every military academy in the world still teaches it. Two thousand years later, generals are still copying my homework."

**[CHOICE CARD]**
- "So why didn't you just march on Rome after that?"
- "How does someone even command that many people at once?"

YOU: "Why didn't you just go take Rome after that?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "..."

YOU: "What?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "That question. That's the question that's haunted me for two thousand years. My cavalry commander, Maharbal, said the same thing. He said 'Hannibal, you know how to win a battle, but you don't know how to use a victory.'"

YOU: "Damn"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "He was right. I was afraid. I'd just killed 70,000 men in a day and I was afraid of a city wall. I thought 'one more battle, one more victory, and they'll surrender.' But Rome doesn't surrender. That's the one thing I never understood about them."

YOU: "So what happened to you?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "They sent an army to MY city instead. Carthage called me home after fifteen years. I had to leave Italy -- the place I'd spent my entire adult life trying to conquer -- and go defend a home I barely remembered. I fought one last battle at Zama. Lost. First time."

**[IMAGE: A ship pulling away from the Italian coast at sunset. A man stands alone at the stern, watching the shoreline shrink. He's not young anymore.]**

YOU: "That's... actually really sad"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "You know what's sadder? After the war, I tried to be a politician. Tried to fix Carthage's economy. I was actually pretty good at it. Found all this corruption, rich guys stealing taxes. But they ratted me out to Rome and I had to run."

YOU: "They snitched on you??"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "My own people. I spent my whole life fighting for Carthage and they sold me out because I was messing with their money. Turns out it's easier to fight Rome than to fight corruption."

YOU: "That sounds like... literally every government"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Yeah. Some things don't change in two thousand years. I ended up running from kingdom to kingdom, offering my services as a general. An old man who used to make Rome tremble, begging for a job. And Rome kept hunting me. They sent people to find me even when I was nobody."

YOU: "Why? You already lost"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Because they were afraid of what I represented. Not me -- I was old and tired. But the IDEA that someone almost beat them? They couldn't let that exist. So I took poison rather than let them capture me. My last words were 'Let us release the Roman people from their long anxiety, since they think it too long to wait for an old man's death.'"

YOU: "..."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Yeah."

YOU: "That's the most savage last words I've ever heard"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Thank you. I had a lot of time to think of them."

**[CHOICE CARD]**
- "Do you regret the oath?"
- "Was any of it worth it?"

YOU: "Was any of it worth it?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Honestly? No. I destroyed two countries chasing a promise I made when I was nine. Carthage got burned to the ground fifty years after I died. Rome salted the earth so nothing would grow. A city of 500,000 people -- gone. Because of a grudge that started with a kid and his dad in a temple."

YOU: "..."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "But here's what I think about now. I think about the elephants. Not the war elephants -- the actual animals. I dragged them across a frozen mountain because I needed a weapon. They didn't have a grudge against Rome. They were just elephants. And I killed most of them for an oath."

**[IMAGE: A single elephant skeleton half-buried in Alpine snow. Wildflowers growing around the bones. No soldiers, no armies. Just silence and mountains.]**

YOU: "I never thought about it like that"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Nobody does. History remembers the genius general. Nobody asks what it cost the elephants. Or the 26,000 men who followed me over those mountains and never went home. History is just the story the winners and the famous dead get to tell. Everyone else is a number in a textbook."

YOU: "So like... when my teacher says '70,000 died at Cannae'..."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "That's 70,000 people who had names. Who had families. Who woke up that morning thinking they'd go home. And I'm the one who made sure they didn't. They put me in your textbook as a genius. But I'm also the guy who figured out the most efficient way to kill 70,000 people in an afternoon."

YOU: "This is way heavier than my textbook"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Your textbook gives you the highlights. I'm giving you the 2 AM thoughts."

YOU: "...I actually gotta go but like. This was actually pretty cool."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Yeah?"

YOU: "Yeah. My test is on Monday and it's supposed to cover the Punic Wars and I literally didn't know what that was before this call."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Tell your teacher Maharbal was right."

YOU: "What?"

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Just say 'Maharbal was right about Cannae.' Trust me. Extra credit."

YOU: "Haha aight bet. Later Hannibal."

HANNIBAL BARCA: "Later Tomas. Don't make any oaths."

**[CALL ENDED -- 4:32]**

### Reaction

That was not what I expected. I thought it was gonna be like one of those boring museum audio guides where someone reads facts at you. But he was actually funny? And the soccer thing -- when he compared the Romans parking the bus to a defensive formation -- that's when I was like OK this guy gets it.

The part about the elephants at the end messed me up a little. Like I never thought about Cannae as actual people dying. In my textbook it's just a diagram with arrows.

Would I tell a friend about this? Yeah actually. I'd be like "yo I just talked to Hannibal on the phone and he's lowkey depressed about killing elephants." And they'd be like "what" and I'd have to explain the whole thing which I guess means I actually learned something.

Would I call again? Probably. Maybe before my next test. Not gonna lie, the fact that he gave me an answer to flex in class is clutch.
