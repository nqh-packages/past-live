## Diego (13, ADHD creative) -- Genghis Khan

8th grader, diagnosed ADHD, loves drawing and comics, history is his worst subject. Medication wears off by study time. Hyperfocuses on things he likes. Textbooks put him to sleep.

---

[RING... RING...]

[CLICK]

GENGHIS KHAN: "You're calling ME? Ha. Most people ran the other direction."

YOU: "Wait -- you actually picked up??"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I pick up for everyone. That's how I built an empire. You think I conquered half the world by ignoring people? No. I LISTENED. Then I decided if they were useful or... not."

YOU: "That's... kinda terrifying."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Good. It should be. But relax -- you're thirteen. I'm not recruiting. Yet."

[IMAGE: A frozen steppe at dawn. A tiny camp of maybe five tents, surrounded by nothing. Snow everywhere. A skinny kid -- maybe 10 -- is holding a bow that's almost bigger than he is.]

YOU: "Wait is that YOU?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "Temujin. That was my name before I was Genghis Khan. Before anything. My father had just been poisoned by a rival clan. My family got kicked out -- my mother, my brothers, me. We ate rats. RATS. Marmots if we were lucky. You ever eat a marmot?"

YOU: "No??"

GENGHIS KHAN: "Don't. Anyway -- we were nobodies. Less than nobodies. The other clans left us to die. They figured the steppe would finish us off."

YOU: "But it didn't."

GENGHIS KHAN: "No. It did NOT. And you know what saved me? Not being the strongest. Not being the smartest. Being the angriest. I had this... FIRE. Someone wrongs you that badly, you either break or you burn. I burned."

YOU: "Dude that's literally a villain origin story--"

GENGHIS KHAN: "HERO origin story. Depends who's writing it."

[IMAGE: Two teenage boys on horseback in tall grass. One has a stolen horse by the reins. The other -- Temujin -- is grinning like he just pulled off the heist of a lifetime. Behind them, distant riders are chasing.]

YOU: "DID YOU STEAL A HORSE??"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I RECOVERED a horse. It was stolen FROM me first. A man named Belgutei's brother took our horses -- all of them. Without horses on the steppe, you're dead. So yes, I went and got them back. And the boy who helped me chase those thieves? His name was Bo'orchu. He became my first general. My first REAL friend."

YOU: "You met your best friend stealing horses back??"

GENGHIS KHAN: "The best friendships start with crimes. Write that down."

YOU: "I'm DEFINITELY drawing this scene later."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Good. Make me look handsome. The Chinese paintings all made me look like a potato."

YOU: "Okay okay but -- so you went from eating rats to conquering EVERYTHING. How??"

GENGHIS KHAN: "One rule. ONE rule that nobody else on the steppe followed."

YOU: "What?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I promoted people because they were GOOD. Not because of their family. Not their clan. Not their bloodline. If a shepherd's son was brilliant -- he became a commander. If a prince was stupid -- he carried water. THAT was the revolution. Not the horses, not the bows. Meritocracy."

YOU: "That's... actually kind of fair?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "It was INSANELY fair. And it made everyone else furious. The old chiefs? They HATED me. 'How dare this rat-eating orphan put commoners above us?' But my commoners fought like demons because they had something to fight FOR."

[IMAGE: A massive Mongol army stretched across a valley -- but instead of chaos, they're in perfect formations, small units with colored flags. Riders carrying signal arrows. It looks more like a machine than a mob.]

YOU: "That looks like an RTS game. Like Age of Empires."

GENGHIS KHAN: "I don't know what that is but I'll take the compliment. Each unit -- ten men. Ten units -- one hundred. Ten hundreds -- one thousand. Decimal system. Clean. Fast. Every soldier knew exactly who to follow."

YOU: "Wait you invented THAT?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I organized it. And here's the trick -- I MIXED the clans. On purpose. You're not fighting next to your cousins anymore. You're fighting next to strangers who became brothers. Loyalty to ME, not to your uncle."

YOU: "That's... actually genius."

[CHOICE CARD]

GENGHIS KHAN: "Now -- a question for you. This is real. This happened. I've united the Mongol tribes. I'm about to face the Khwarezmian Empire. Massive. Rich. Walled cities. Three times my numbers. Their sultan just MURDERED my trade ambassadors -- chopped their heads off and sent them back to me as a message."

What do you do?

A) "Go full rage mode. Invade immediately. They killed your people."

B) "Send one more diplomat. Give them ONE chance to apologize."

C) "Find their weaknesses first. Scout. Plan. Be patient."

YOU: "C. Definitely C. You don't just run in, that's how you die in every game--"

GENGHIS KHAN: "HA! You think like a Mongol. That is EXACTLY what I did. I sent spies. Traders. I mapped every city, every water source, every grain store. I learned that the sultan's own generals hated him. His empire was rotting from the inside. So when I finally came..."

[IMAGE: The walls of a massive city -- Bukhara -- with Mongol riders circling it like wolves. Smoke rising. But the gates are OPENING from the inside. Citizens are surrendering before the fight even starts.]

GENGHIS KHAN: "...they opened the doors for me. Not all of them. But enough. Bukhara fell in days. A city that should have held for months."

YOU: "Because you knew it was weak inside."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Intelligence wins wars. Not muscles. Remember that when someone bigger than you picks a fight at school."

YOU: "Oh trust me I will."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Now -- I need to be honest with you about something."

YOU: "...okay?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "What came next was... not heroic. I burned cities. Millions died. I won't lie to you about that. When a city resisted -- when they killed my ambassadors or my soldiers -- I made an example. A terrible example. I wanted the NEXT city to surrender without fighting."

YOU: "Did it work?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "...Yes. That's the worst part. It worked. Terror as strategy. The next ten cities surrendered immediately. Saved millions of lives by destroying one. That math -- that HORRIBLE math -- I still think about it."

[Silence for a moment]

YOU: "Do you feel bad about it?"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I feel... complicated about it. I built the largest empire in human history. I connected East and West. The Silk Road was SAFE under me -- a woman could walk from Korea to Hungary carrying gold and nobody would touch her. I created religious freedom -- worship whoever you want, I don't care. I created a postal system, a legal code, diplomatic immunity. But I also left pyramids of skulls outside cities that defied me. Both things are true. History is not comfortable."

YOU: "That's... yeah. My textbook just says 'Mongol hordes' and moves on."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Of course it does. The losers wrote those books. And I am not a simple story."

[CHOICE CARD]

GENGHIS KHAN: "Last question. I'm old now. I've conquered everything from China to Persia. My empire is the biggest the world has ever seen. I need to decide: what happens when I die?"

What would you choose?

A) "Give it all to your oldest son. Traditional."

B) "Split it between your sons equally."

C) "Pick the most competent son, even if he's not the oldest."

YOU: "B feels like it would cause problems... A is boring... C? You said you promoted by skill, not blood--"

GENGHIS KHAN: "I went with B. And you know what? It DID cause problems. Eventually. My sons fought. My grandsons fought worse. The empire split into four pieces within two generations. Maybe you're right -- maybe I should have picked ONE. But... they were my SONS. Even conquerors are fathers."

YOU: "...That's actually really sad."

GENGHIS KHAN: "It's HUMAN. That's what your textbook won't tell you. I was human. Flawed. Brilliant and brutal and human."

[IMAGE: An old man on horseback, looking out over an endless green plain. The Mongolian steppe. No army. No cities. Just grass and sky and one man who changed the entire world, sitting quietly.]

GENGHIS KHAN: "Draw that one for me, artist."

YOU: "...I will. I actually will."

GENGHIS KHAN: "Good. And next time your teacher says 'Mongol hordes' -- raise your hand and tell them about Bo'orchu. About the stolen horses. About the rat-eating orphan who changed everything."

YOU: "I'm gonna get in trouble."

GENGHIS KHAN: "The best people always do. Goodbye, Diego."

[CLICK]

## Reaction

I just wrote that whole thing in like twenty minutes and I didn't even notice. I'm supposed to be doing my math homework. But I literally want to draw that last scene RIGHT NOW -- the old man on the horse with the empty steppe. And the horse-stealing one. And the army formations.

That's the thing -- if Past, Live actually felt like THAT? Like talking to someone who's funny and real and doesn't lecture you but also doesn't hide the dark stuff? I would use it every single night. I would call every historical figure on that list. I would know more history than my teacher.

The choices are what keep me locked in. The second it said "what do you do" my brain went from listening mode to GAMING mode. I was strategizing. I was thinking about consequences. That's not studying. That's PLAYING. And I learned more in five minutes than a whole chapter of my textbook.

The images are key too. I need to SEE it. The second I saw that kid holding the bow on the frozen steppe -- that's when it stopped being "history" and became a STORY. I want to draw every single one of those scenes.

What would make me hang up: if he started listing dates. If he talked like a Wikipedia article. If he was too nice and didn't mention the skull pyramids. I want REAL. Real means complicated. Real means uncomfortable. Real means interesting.

What would make me call back: more choices. Harder choices. Choices where I'm wrong sometimes and the character tells me what actually happened and WHY. Consequences. Like a visual novel but it's real history.

I would 100% come back. Every night. This is the first time history felt like it was FOR me.
