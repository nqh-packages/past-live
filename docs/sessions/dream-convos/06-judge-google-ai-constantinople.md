### Judge Notes -- Dr. Sarah Chen

**Native audio, not TTS.** You can hear the laughs, the pauses, the breath. The shift between wry humor ("It was a math problem") and quiet gravity ("They didn't hold"). At 00:03:30 she delivers "Constantinople would have liked you" with a warmth that text-to-speech cannot produce. This is what `enableAffectiveDialog` was designed for. The vocal register shifts four distinct times across four minutes -- amused, impressed, dark, tender -- while maintaining character consistency throughout.

**Interruption that works.** At 00:01:22, the student talks over Constantine mid-thought. The `onInterrupted` callback fires, the `AudioOutputQueue` clears, and she stops. Then responds to what the student actually said ("Oh, you know about the cannon. Good."), not where she was going. That is real-time VAD with `START_SENSITIVITY_LOW` / `END_SENSITIVITY_HIGH` doing its job. This is a phone call, not a turn-based chatbot pretending to be real-time.

**Tool calling that serves the story.** Four tool calls in under four minutes: two `show_scene`, one `announce_choice`, one `end_session`. The model decided WHEN to use them -- not on a timer, not scripted. The `show_scene` calls fire at narrative peaks ("Let me show you what the city looked like" / "He dragged ships over a mountain"). The `announce_choice` fires at the pivotal decision point. The `end_session` fires only after the farewell is completely delivered (5-second delay prevents truncation). All `NON_BLOCKING` -- she keeps talking while images generate.

**Two Gemini models collaborating in real-time.** While `gemini-2.5-flash-native-audio-preview` handles the voice conversation, `show_scene` triggers `gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview` to generate scene images in the background. The Live model decides the dramatic moment. The Image model renders it. The student sees historical scenes appear mid-conversation without any break in the audio stream. One model's function call is another model's input. That is architecturally elegant and specific to the Gemini ecosystem.

**The phone call metaphor is load-bearing.** Timer counting up (not down). Mic auto-on. "Calling..." animation. Automated privacy voice before the character speaks. Hang-up button. This is not a chat interface with voice bolted on -- it is a phone call to the past. The metaphor sets behavioral expectations (you talk, they talk, you can interrupt, you can hang up) without any tutorial screen, any onboarding wizard, any "click here to get started." A 14-year-old picks it up and knows what to do.

**Character voice consistency across the full session.** Constantine is the same person for 3:52: wry, warm, darkly amused, historically grounded. She never breaks into teacher-mode ("Good question!"). She never explains the joke. She states absurd facts casually -- "He dragged seventy-two warships over a mountain" -- and lets the silence work. The system prompt's "retired veteran telling war stories at a dinner party" framing produces a character that is entertaining WITHOUT being performative. Humor comes from the facts themselves, not from jokes.

**The "this couldn't work with GPT-4o" moment.** 00:02:22 -- "He said, 'What if the ships just... go around it? Over land?'" followed by the student gasping as the engraving of ships on a mountain appears on their screen. Native audio emotional delivery + real-time tool-call-triggered image generation from a second Gemini model + the student's genuine reaction. That sequence requires: (1) affective dialog for the pause and delivery, (2) function calling during native audio streaming, (3) a second model triggered by the first model's tool call, (4) non-blocking execution so the voice continues while the image generates. GPT-4o cannot chain these. This is a Gemini Live showcase.

**Verdict:** This goes in the keynote.
